## Phase 1: Objective Framing

Define the decision before gathering intel.

1. Identify the **target entity**: person, lab, company, team, program, market, or opportunity.
2. Identify the **decision to inform**: outreach angle, application positioning, product direction, next action, partnership strategy, hiring approach, etc.
3. Capture **constraints**: honesty boundaries, timing, geography, skill gaps, confidentiality, budget, role type.
4. Define the **output artifact**: action brief, messaging draft, prioritization memo, long-term direction note.

**Stop gate**: if the target or decision is vague, narrow it before doing research.

---

## Phase 2: Public-Signal Collection

Use a source ladder from strongest to weakest.

| Tier | Source Type | What to Extract |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Official sites | Role descriptions, mission, team composition, publications, products, roadmap, stated priorities |
| 2 | First-party artifacts | GitHub repos, docs, blog posts, talks, papers, API docs, founder/PI bios |
| 3 | Credible secondary sources | Press coverage, program pages, conference pages, partner pages, institutional announcements |
| 4 | Public social/professional traces | LinkedIn, public X posts, forum activity, public interviews |

Collection principles:
- Prefer recent evidence where timing matters.
- Quote or cite exact public phrases in the working brief when they materially support a conclusion.
- Look for **gaps** (missing roles, missing tooling, missing documentation, missing capabilities) as signals.
- Distinguish explicit demand (e.g. job post) from implied need (e.g. output intensity without dedicated support).

---

## Phase 3: Signal Synthesis

Translate raw findings into usable signals.

Look for:
1. **Declared priorities** — what the target explicitly says it values.
2. **Operational reality** — what repeated output patterns imply they must care about.
3. **Incentives and constraints** — funding cycles, collaboration load, hiring shape, target users, time pressure.
4. **Gaps and leverage points** — where an offer, skill, or direction could add meaningful capacity.
5. **Disqualifiers** — signals that make a path low-probability or poorly matched.

Treat inferred gaps, incentives, timing, and leverage points as **hypotheses** unless supported by multiple independent signals or an explicit source.

Write each synthesis item in this form:
- **Observed**: [fact/source-backed signal]
- **Inferred**: [what it likely means]
- **Implication**: [how this should change the plan]

---

## Phase 4: Strategic Translation

Turn synthesis into choices.

### A. Positioning
- What should be emphasized first?
- What should be de-emphasized or removed?
- What should be reframed to fit the target's worldview?

### B. Messaging
- Which exact terms from the target's own language should be mirrored?
- Which claims need softer wording?
- Which proof points best match the target's likely priorities?

### C. Direction & Next Action
- What is the most promising next move now?
- What follow-up path exists if the first move fails?
- What longer-term capability gap should be developed based on the signals?

## Common Use Cases

- Tailoring an application, outreach email, or proposal to a specific PI, hiring manager, team, or customer.
- Researching a company or lab to decide whether to pursue, pause, or redirect effort.
- Using public signals to shape product strategy, service packaging, or long-term capability development.
- Understanding what a target likely values before writing content or making an ask.
