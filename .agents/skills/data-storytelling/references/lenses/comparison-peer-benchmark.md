# Lens: Comparison - Peer Benchmark

## Purpose

Compare performance to peers, competitors, or external reference points.

## Best For

- Market context.
- Executive or board narratives.
- Understanding whether a change is internal or industry-wide.

## Required Inputs

- Metric.
- External benchmark or peer reference.
- Comparable time window and definitions.

## Questions Answered

- Are we outperforming or lagging peers?
- Is the issue market-wide or specific to us?
- How large is the benchmark gap?

## Method

1. Validate metric definition parity.
2. Compare absolute performance and trend direction.
3. State whether the benchmark is internal, peer, or industry reference.

## Output Contract

- `baseline_or_comparator`: <reported peer set, benchmark source, or external reference used>
- `method_parameters`: <reported benchmark normalization, comparability adjustments, and any definition harmonization used>
- Benchmark source.
- Gap versus benchmark.
- Whether the difference is narrowing or widening.
- Caveat on comparability.

## Caveats

- Benchmarks are often mismatched in definition or freshness.
- Never hide comparability issues.
